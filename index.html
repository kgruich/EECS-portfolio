KO Kode: My Custom Game Engine

  • KO Kode is a 2D multiplatform engine with extensive support for input methods and customization.
  • The engine is coded in C++, hosts Lua scripting, and utiizes GLM, LuaBridge, Rapidjson, and SDL2 to do so.
  • Watch a demonstration of the engine here!.

The Legend of Zelda (Unity Remaster)

  • For the first milestone while creating this Unity remaster, I did a lot of work on the movement and room system. This also included work on player collection UI and locked doors.
  • For the alpha, I focused on designing the enemy AI for our various NPCs, and laying out the rooms to match the original Zelda.
  • For the gold build, I designed our custom mechanic: the bouncing fireball weapon and the exploding fireball weapon. This, along with the aiming, and fixing many of the inauthentic parts of our previous build was my focus.

A Tower Defense Prototype

  • For the first build of this game, I actually did it all in 3D, but that posed various issues for the generation of chunks, requiring a lot of bespoke tile generation that was beyond my scope.
  • For the final build, I decided to restart in 2D. This worked out very well, allowing for good looking tilemaps, simple collisions, 2D sprites instead of 3D models, several enemies and towers.
  • While I had grander ideas and wanted to develop an expanding map, I am satisfied with what I have accomplished in rapidly making a fun tower defense protoype.
